The Problem

Driver availability varied widely across neighborhoods, leading to significant operational inefficiencies. The business needed a city-level, time-specific view of where fulfillment was failing.

  • Unpredictable wait times for customers in specific zones.
  • Missed or delayed trips due to lack of driver coverage.
  • Inefficient driver allocation resulting in idle drivers in low-demand areas.
  • Localized demand surges that weren‚Äôt visible in standard aggregate reports.

The dashboard above visualizes these gaps, enabling teams to monitor performance in real time and adjust quickly.

02

My Approach in Tableau

1. Custom Geocoding & Spatial Setup

Imported latitude/longitude and administrative boundaries. Created custom geocoded regions for neighborhoods not supported natively and built polygon maps to accurately reflect real Nairobi geography.

2. Hotspot Mapping (Choropleth)

Colored each zone by fulfillment rate (Green = strong supply, Yellow = moderate, Red = low). Enabled hover interactions showing hourly patterns and KPIs.

3. Hour-by-Hour Heat Grid

Built a 24-hour timeline heatmap for every region, allowing quick scanning of peak failure times (e.g., 7‚Äì9 AM, 5‚Äì8 PM) using color encoding.

4. Dashboard Interactivity

Implemented region selection to filter heatmaps, tooltips with fulfillment %, demand, and driver count, and linked spatial and temporal views.
