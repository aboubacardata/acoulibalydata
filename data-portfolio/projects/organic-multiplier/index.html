01

Business Problem

The "Black Box" of Organic Growth

The growth team understood their "Paid" and "Returning" user metrics, but "New Organic Users" (free acquisition) was treated as a random variable. The business lacked a quantitative method to understand if marketing spend and retention efforts were actually driving word-of-mouth (WOM) referrals, or if organic growth was simply coincidental.

Objective: Deconstruct the drivers of organic traffic to calculate a "WOM Coefficient" and predict how Paid/Returning users generate free new users.

02

Key Hypotheses Tested

The Flywheel Effect

An increase in Paid and Returning users directly causes a lift in New Organic Users (satisfied users bring friends).

Predictability

The relationship between active user base and new organic signups is linear and statistically significant.

Granularity Matters

Modeling data on a daily level yields more accurate predictive power than weekly or monthly aggregates.

Adopt "WOM" as a KPI
