01

Business Problem

The Latency Challenge

The lending platform faced a critical operational inefficiency: average first-loan processing times had doubled from 20 to 40 hours, threatening customer experience and portfolio health. The business lacked a quantitative understanding of how these delays impacted repayment, churn, and re-application rates.

Objective: Diagnose the root causes of latency and determine the optimal speed required to maximize portfolio performance.

02

Key Hypotheses Tested

Disbursement Speed vs. Risk

Faster loan disbursement correlates with higher repayment rates and lower default risk.

Churn Sensitivity

Longer processing wait times directly increase the loan cancellation rate.

Operational Bottlenecks

Specific stages in the manual due diligence process are the primary drivers of the time increase.

Segmentation Bias

Loan size and specific sectors disproportionately affect wait times, disadvantaging certain customer segments.

04

Recommended Actions

Establish Operational SLAs

  • Implement a strict 17-hour Service Level Agreement (SLA) for first loans to capitalize on the higher repayment rates observed in this window.

Automate Low-Value Tickets

  • Restructure the workflow for small principal loans to minimize manual touchpoints and reverse the high-latency trend.

Fix the "Resubmission" Loop

  • Redesign the verification workflow to eliminate the 16-hour lag, specifically addressing the lack of actionability on weekend inputs.

Product Bug Resolution

  • Collaborated with Product to fix a routing logic error where incomplete applications were being incorrectly routed to the wrong review stage.
